Rabies Vaccination Schedule in India

India follows the 4-dose Essen regimen for post-exposure prophylaxis (PEP) against rabies. This is the standard protocol recommended by the National Centre for Disease Control (NCDC) and the World Health Organization (WHO) for immunocompetent individuals bitten or scratched by a suspected rabid animal.

What to Do After a Dog Bite or Animal Scratch

1. Wash the wound immediately with soap and running water for at least 15 minutes.

2. Visit a doctor or hospital immediately — do not wait.

3. Start vaccination on Day 0 — the same day as the bite if possible.

4. For Category III exposures (deep bites, scratches, or licks on broken skin), Rabies Immunoglobulin (RIG) must also be administered on Day 0.

5. Complete all 4 doses — missing doses significantly reduces protection.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many rabies injections are needed in India?

4 injections are required for a standard course in India — given on Days 0, 3, 7, and 28 after the first dose.

What is the rabies vaccine schedule after a dog bite in India?

Following a dog bite, the anti-rabies vaccine is given on Day 0 (immediately), Day 3, Day 7, and Day 28. This is the Essen regimen used across India.

What if I miss a rabies vaccine dose?

Take the missed dose as soon as possible and continue the remaining doses on the original schedule. Do not restart the series. Consult your doctor if you are unsure.

Is rabies 100% fatal without vaccination?

Yes — once symptoms appear, rabies is almost always fatal. This is why starting PEP immediately after exposure is critical. Timely vaccination is fully effective in preventing the disease.

Do I need rabies immunoglobulin (RIG) along with the vaccine?

RIG is required for Category III exposures (deep bites, puncture wounds, licks on mucous membranes or broken skin). It is given only once on Day 0, alongside the first vaccine dose.
